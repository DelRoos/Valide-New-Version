# Chinese arts and traditions

Chinese civilisation, one of the oldest in the world, has produced an artistic and cultural heritage of exceptional richness. From calligraphy to Peking Opera, through martial arts and silk, these traditions embody deep philosophical values linked to Confucianism, Taoism and Buddhism.

## Calligraphy (书法 shūfǎ)

:::definition
**书法 shūfǎ** (calligraphy) is considered the foremost art in China. It is the art of writing Chinese characters with a brush, seeking beauty of form and expression of the author's character.

**The Four Treasures of the Study** (文房四宝 wénfáng sìbǎo):

| Treasure | Character | Pinyin | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| The brush | 笔 | bǐ | Writing instrument (wolf, sheep hair...) |
| The ink | 墨 | mò | Ink stick to be ground on the stone |
| The paper | 纸 | zhǐ | Invented in China (Cai Lun, 105 CE) |
| The ink stone | 砚 | yàn | Tray on which ink is ground |

**The five great calligraphic styles:**

| Style | Character | Pinyin | Period | Characteristic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Oracle bone | 甲骨文 | jiǎgǔwén | Shang (~1200 BCE) | Carved on bones and tortoise shells |
| Seal script | 篆书 | zhuànshū | Qin-Han | Rounded forms, ancient official use |
| Clerical script | 隶书 | lìshū | Han | Transition towards rectangular forms |
| Regular script | 楷书 | kǎishū | Tang | Current standard style, readable and balanced |
| Cursive script | 草书 | cǎoshū | All | Fast writing, almost illegible, very expressive |
:::

## Peking Opera (京剧 Jīngjù)

:::propriete
**京剧 Jīngjù** (Peking Opera) has been on the **UNESCO intangible cultural heritage list since 2010**. It is a total theatrical form combining singing, acrobatics, choreographed combat and elaborate costumes.

**The four main roles:**

| Role | Character | Pinyin | Type of character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sheng | 生 | shēng | Main male character (hero, scholar) |
| Dan | 旦 | dàn | Female character (formerly played by men) |
| Jing | 净 | jìng | Male character with painted face (general, god, judge) |
| Chou | 丑 | chǒu | Clown, comic character with white patch on the nose |

**The face-paint colour code (脸谱 liǎnpǔ):**

| Colour | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Red | Loyalty, courage, virtue |
| White | Treachery, cunning, deceitfulness |
| Black | Honesty, uprightness (just but stern character) |
| Yellow | Cruelty, violence |
| Gold / Silver | Divine, immortal or demonic characters |
:::

## Martial arts (武术 wǔshù)

:::methode
**武术 wǔshù** (martial arts, popularised in the West as "kung-fu" 功夫 gōngfū) is a set of martial disciplines born more than 2,500 years ago.

**Two main families:**
- **External style** (外家 wàijiā): physical strength, speed, striking — e.g. 少林功夫 Shàolín gōngfū (Shaolin)
- **Internal style** (内家 nèijiā): inner energy (气 qì), flexibility, fluidity — e.g. 太极拳 Tàijíquán (Tai chi)

**太极拳 Tàijíquán (Tai chi):**
- Practised by millions of Chinese people every morning in parks.
- Slow and fluid sequences symbolising the balance of yin (阴 yīn) / yang (阳 yáng).
- Listed on UNESCO heritage in 2020.
- Benefits: balance, concentration, health for older people.

The **Shaolin Temple** (少林寺 Shàolín Sì) in Henan province is the birthplace of Buddhist kung-fu — monks have been practising since the 6th century CE.
:::

## Silk, porcelain and paper — major Chinese inventions

:::propriete
China is behind many inventions that changed the world:

| Invention | Character | Pinyin | Period | World impact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Silk | 丝绸 | sīchóu | ~2700 BCE | World trade, the Silk Road |
| Paper | 纸 | zhǐ | 105 CE | Revolution in writing and knowledge |
| Printing | 印刷术 | yìnshuāshù | 868 CE (woodblock) | Large-scale spread of knowledge |
| Compass | 指南针 | zhǐnán zhēn | 10th century | Global maritime navigation |
| Gunpowder | 火药 | huǒyào | 9th century | Warfare, then civilian fireworks |
| Porcelain | 瓷器 | cíqì | 5th-6th century | "China" = English name for Chinese porcelain |

**Silk** has been cultivated since the legend of Empress 嫘祖 Léizǔ (2700 BCE) who reportedly discovered the silkworm cocoon when it fell into her tea. The secret of its production was kept for 3,000 years on pain of death.
:::

:::exemple
**Dialogue at a museum in Beijing:**

Guide: 这是京剧的脸谱。你知道红色代表什么吗？
        Zhè shì jīngjù de liǎnpǔ. Nǐ zhīdào hóngsè dàibiǎo shénme ma?
        This is the face-paint of Peking Opera. Do you know what red represents?

Amara: 代表忠诚吧？我在书上看到过。
        Dàibiǎo zhōngchéng ba? Wǒ zài shū shàng kàn dào guò.
        Loyalty, right? I read about it in a book.

Guide: 对！红色象征忠诚和勇气。白色呢？
        Duì! Hóngsè xiàngzhēng zhōngchéng hé yǒngqì. Báisè ne?
        Correct! Red symbolises loyalty and courage. And white?

Amara: 白色是奸诈的意思，对吗？
        Báisè shì jiānzhà de yìsi, duì ma?
        White means treachery, doesn't it?

Guide: 非常好！你的中文很棒！
        Fēicháng hǎo! Nǐ de Zhōngwén hěn bàng!
        Excellent! Your Chinese is great!
:::

:::retenir
- 书法 shūfǎ (calligraphy): foremost art in China — brush 笔, ink 墨, paper 纸, stone 砚.
- 5 styles: 甲骨文 (oracle bone), 篆书 (seal), 隶书 (clerical), 楷书 (regular), 草书 (cursive).
- 京剧 Jīngjù (Peking Opera): UNESCO 2010 — 4 roles: 生 (hero), 旦 (female), 净 (painted face), 丑 (clown).
- Colours: red = loyalty, white = treachery, black = honesty.
- 太极拳 Tàijíquán (Tai chi): UNESCO 2020 — internal arts, yin/yang.
- Great inventions: silk, paper, printing, compass, gunpowder, porcelain.
:::
