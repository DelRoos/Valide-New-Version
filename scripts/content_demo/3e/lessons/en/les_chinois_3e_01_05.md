# Colours, adjectives and negation

Being able to describe the world around us is essential in Chinese. In this lesson, we learn colours, the position of adjectives in a sentence, and the two negation particles — one of the most important grammatical distinctions in Mandarin.

## Colours (颜色 yánsè)

:::propriete
| Colour | Character | Pinyin | Cultural note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Red | 红色 | hóngsè | Symbol of luck, happiness and prosperity |
| Orange | 橙色 | chéngsè | |
| Yellow | 黄色 | huángsè | Imperial colour (emperors) |
| Green | 绿色 | lǜsè | Nature, hope, environment |
| Blue | 蓝色 | lánsè | Sky, sea |
| Purple | 紫色 | zǐsè | Nobility, spirituality |
| White | 白色 | báisè | ⚠️ Mourning and funerals in China |
| Black | 黑色 | hēisè | |
| Grey | 灰色 | huīsè | |
| Pink | 粉色 | fěnsè | Femininity |
| Brown | 棕色 | zōngsè | |

**Note**: the suffix **色 sè** (colour) is usually added to the colour adjective. In everyday speech, 色 can be omitted: 红的 hóng de (the red one).
:::

:::attention
In China, **white** is the colour of mourning (funerals, illness). Do not offer white flowers to a host, or wear white to a celebration. This is the opposite of many Western countries where white is the colour of weddings.

**Red** is the colour of happiness — always appropriate for festivals, gifts and weddings.
:::

## Adjectives and sentence structure

:::definition
In Mandarin, adjectives can be used in two ways:

1. **As a predicate** (no verb 'to be'): Subject + 很 hěn + adjective
   - 这本书很好。Zhè běn shū hěn hǎo. = This book is good.
   - 很 hěn (very) is required before a predicate adjective to avoid a comparative meaning.

2. **As an attributive** (before a noun): adjective + 的 de + noun
   - 红色的包 hóngsè de bāo = the red bag
   - 漂亮的女孩 piàoliang de nǚhái = a beautiful girl
:::

:::propriete
**Common descriptive adjectives:**

| Adjective | Character | Pinyin | Opposite | Pinyin |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Big | 大 | dà | Small | 小 xiǎo |
| Long | 长 | cháng | Short | 短 duǎn |
| Hot | 热 | rè | Cold | 冷 lěng |
| Beautiful | 漂亮 | piàoliang | Ugly | 丑 chǒu |
| New | 新 | xīn | Old | 旧 jiù |
| Fast | 快 | kuài | Slow | 慢 màn |
| Easy | 容易 | róngyì | Difficult | 难 nán |
| Interesting | 有趣 | yǒuqù | Boring | 无聊 wúliáo |
:::

## Negation — 不 bù and 没 méi

:::definition
Mandarin has **two distinct negation particles** depending on context:

**1. 不 bù** — negates present, future, habits and adjectives
**2. 没 méi** — negates past actions or the verb 有 yǒu (to have/there is)
:::

:::propriete
| Context | Particle | Example | Translation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Present / future action | 不 bù | 我不去 wǒ bù qù | I'm not going (now/later) |
| Habit / refusal | 不 bù | 我不吃辣 wǒ bù chī là | I don't eat spicy food (habit) |
| Identity / state | 不 bù | 我不是学生 wǒ bú shì xuéshēng | I am not a student |
| Adjective | 不 bù | 不好 bù hǎo | not good |
| Past action | 没 méi | 我没去 wǒ méi qù | I didn't go |
| Possession / existence | 没有 méiyǒu | 我没有钱 wǒ méiyǒu qián | I have no money |
| Past experience | 没 méi | 我没吃过日本菜 | I've never eaten Japanese food |
:::

:::methode
**How to choose between 不 and 没?**

1. Is it a **past** action? → use **没** méi
   - 我没去学校。Wǒ méi qù xuéxiào. = I didn't go to school. (yesterday)

2. Is it a negation of **to have (有 yǒu)**? → use **没有** méiyǒu
   - 我没有作业。Wǒ méiyǒu zuòyè. = I don't have any homework.

3. Everything else (present, future, adjective, habit, state) → use **不** bù
   - 我不去。Wǒ bù qù. = I'm not going. (now)
   - 这不好吃。Zhè bù hǎo chī. = This is not tasty.
:::

:::exemple
Translate into Mandarin:

1. I am not going to school tomorrow.
   → 我明天**不**去学校。Wǒ míngtiān bù qù xuéxiào.

2. I didn't go to school yesterday.
   → 我昨天**没**去学校。Wǒ zuótiān méi qù xuéxiào.

3. I don't have a pen.
   → 我**没有**钢笔。Wǒ méiyǒu gāngbǐ.

4. This bag is not red, it is blue.
   → 这个包**不**是红色的，是蓝色的。
:::

:::retenir
- Main colours: 红(red/luck), 黄(yellow/imperial), 绿(green), 蓝(blue), 白(white/mourning⚠️), 黑(black).
- Predicate adjective: Subject + **很 hěn** + adjective.
- Attributive adjective: adjective + **的 de** + noun.
- **不 bù** = negation of present/future/habits/adjectives.
- **没 méi / 没有 méiyǒu** = negation of past / verb to have.
- Never write 不有 — always **没有** for 'not have'.
:::
