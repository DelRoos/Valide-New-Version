# Shopping and money (购物 gòuwù)

China is the world's largest retail market. From traditional markets (集市 jíshì) to superstores and giant e-commerce platforms (Taobao, JD.com), Chinese people love shopping. In this lesson, we learn to talk about money, ask for prices and negotiate.

## Chinese currency

:::definition
China's official currency is the **人民币 rénmínbì** (RMB), literally meaning 'people's currency'. The main unit is the **元 yuán** (also called 块 kuài in spoken language).

Monetary system structure:
- 1 元 yuán (块 kuài) = 10 角 jiǎo (毛 máo spoken) = 100 分 fēn
- International symbol: **¥** (also used for the Japanese yen)
- In practice, 分 fēn are rarely used today.
:::

:::propriete
**Price examples:**

| Price | Written | Spoken |
|---|---|---|
| 5 yuan | 五元 | wǔ kuài |
| 10 yuan | 十元 | shí kuài |
| 15.50 yuan | 十五元五角 | shíwǔ kuài wǔ máo |
| 100 yuan | 一百元 | yī bǎi kuài |
| 0.50 yuan | 五角 | wǔ máo |

**Digital payment in China**:
- 微信支付 Wēixìn Zhīfù (WeChat Pay) — via the WeChat messaging app
- 支付宝 Zhīfùbǎo (Alipay) — via Alibaba's app
- These two systems cover ~95% of payments in China. Cash (现金 xiànjīn) is increasingly rare. QR codes are displayed everywhere.
:::

## The Chinese discount system (折 zhé)

:::methode
In China, discounts are expressed differently from Western countries:

- **打折 dǎ zhé** = discount (literally 'strike the discount')
- The number represents the **percentage to pay** (not the percentage deducted)

| Expression | Meaning | Western equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 打九折 dǎ jiǔ zhé | pay 90% | 10% off |
| 打八折 dǎ bā zhé | pay 80% | 20% off |
| 打七折 dǎ qī zhé | pay 70% | 30% off |
| 打五折 dǎ wǔ zhé | pay 50% | Half price |

Example: A garment costs 200 yuan and is «打八折» → you pay 200 × 0.8 = **160 yuan**.
:::

:::exemple
**Dialogue at a clothing market:**

Amara: 老板，这件衬衫多少钱？
        Lǎobǎn, zhè jiàn chènshān duōshao qián?
        Boss, how much is this shirt?

Seller: 一百块。
         Yī bǎi kuài.
         100 yuan.

Amara: 太贵了！可以便宜点儿吗？
        Tài guì le! Kěyǐ piányí diǎnr ma?
        Too expensive! Can you go a bit cheaper?

Seller: 好吧，打八折，八十块。
         Hǎo ba, dǎ bā zhé, bāshí kuài.
         Alright, 20% off, 80 yuan.

Amara: 好的，我买了。我要红色的。
        Hǎo de, wǒ mǎi le. Wǒ yào hóngsè de.
        OK, I'll take it. I want the red one.

Seller: 可以用微信支付吗？
         Kěyǐ yòng Wēixìn Zhīfù ma?
         Can you pay with WeChat Pay?
:::

:::attention
Bargaining is common in traditional markets (集市 jíshì) and small shops, but NOT in supermarkets (超市 chāoshì) or department stores (百货公司 bǎihuò gōngsī) where prices are fixed.
:::

:::retenir
- Currency: 人民币 / 元 yuán (块 kuài spoken) = 10 角 jiǎo (毛 máo) = 100 分 fēn.
- Price question: 多少钱？(how much?).
- Bargaining: 太贵了 (too expensive), 便宜点儿 (a bit cheaper).
- Discounts: 打X折 = pay X×10% (打八折 = pay 80%).
- Buying: 我想买... / 我要这个/那个.
- Payment in China: WeChat Pay (微信支付) and Alipay (支付宝) dominate.
:::
