# The pinyin system and tones

Mandarin is the official language of China. To learn how to pronounce it, we use **pinyin**, a writing system that uses Latin letters. But be careful: in Mandarin, the same syllable can have completely different meanings depending on the tone with which it is pronounced!

:::definition
**Mandarin** (普通话 pǔtōnghuà, 'common language') is the official language of the People's Republic of China, Taiwan and Singapore. Spoken by over one billion people, it is the most widely spoken language in the world.

**Pinyin** (拼音 pīnyīn, 'spelling of sounds') is the official romanisation system of Mandarin, created in 1958. It uses Latin alphabet letters supplemented by diacritical marks to indicate tones.
:::

## The 4 tones + the neutral tone

:::propriete
| Tone | Mark | Melodic contour | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1st tone | ā | High and level | High, steady voice | mā 妈 = mother |
| 2nd tone | á | Rising | Voice rises (like asking a question) | má 麻 = hemp |
| 3rd tone | ǎ | Dipping (falling-rising) | Voice falls then rises | mǎ 马 = horse |
| 4th tone | à | Falling | Voice drops sharply | mà 骂 = to scold |
| Neutral tone | a | Short and light | Unstressed syllable, no contour | ma 吗 = question particle |

**Tip**: Visualise the tones as a musical landscape — flat, climb, valley dip, sharp drop.
:::

:::attention
The same sound can have completely different meanings depending on the tone:
- mā (1st) = 妈 mother
- má (2nd) = 麻 hemp
- mǎ (3rd) = 马 horse
- mà (4th) = 骂 to scold

Using the wrong tone can create misunderstandings. Pronouncing tones correctly is **compulsory** in Mandarin.
:::

## The tone rule for 不 (bù)

:::methode
The word 不 bù (not, no) changes tone depending on what follows:

- **Normally**: 不 = 4th tone → bù
- **Before another 4th tone**: 不 becomes 2nd tone → bú

Examples:
- 不好 bù hǎo (not good) — 好 is 3rd tone → 不 stays bù
- 不是 bú shì (it is not) — 是 is 4th tone → 不 becomes bú
- 不去 bú qù (not going) — 去 is 4th tone → 不 becomes bú qù
:::

## Initials (consonants)

:::propriete
| Series | Initials | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Labials | b p m f | b/p = unaspirated / aspirated |
| Dentals | d t n l | d/t = unaspirated / aspirated |
| Velars | g k h | g/k = unaspirated / aspirated |
| Palatals | j q x | only before i and ü |
| Retroflexes | zh ch sh r | tongue curled towards the palate |
| Sibilants | z c s | tongue flat behind the teeth |

**Common pitfalls**: x in pinyin sounds like a soft 'sh' (xièxie ≈ 'shyeh-shyeh'). q sounds like 'tch'.
:::

## Finals (vowels and syllables)

:::propriete
**Simple vowels**: a e i o u ü

**Compound vowels**: ai ei ui ao ou iu ie üe er

**Nasal finals**: an en in un ün ang eng ing ong

**Key examples**:
- 你好 nǐ hǎo = hello (you well → literally 'you in good health')
- 谢谢 xièxiè = thank you
- 再见 zàijiàn = goodbye (again see → 'to meet again')
- 中国 Zhōngguó = China (the middle kingdom)
:::

:::exemple
Read and pronounce these words, respecting the tones:

| Pinyin | Tone(s) | Character | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| nǐ hǎo | 3rd + 3rd | 你好 | hello |
| xièxiè | 4th + neutral | 谢谢 | thank you |
| lǎoshī | 3rd + 1st | 老师 | teacher |
| xuéshēng | 2nd + 1st | 学生 | student |
| Kāmài lóng | 1st + 4th + 2nd | 喀麦隆 | Cameroon |
:::

:::retenir
- Mandarin (普通话 pǔtōnghuà) = official language of China, 1+ billion speakers.
- Pinyin = phonetic transcription using the Latin alphabet, created in 1958.
- **4 tones + 1 neutral tone**: level (ā), rising (á), dipping (ǎ), falling (à).
- Every syllable has a compulsory tone — tones change the meaning of words.
- Rule for 不 bù: becomes bú before a 4th tone.
:::
