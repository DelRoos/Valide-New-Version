# Geography and major cities of China

China is the fourth largest country in the world by area, with an extraordinarily diverse geography: arid deserts, high plateaus, fertile plains, coastlines and tropical forests all coexist on the same territory. Understanding China's geography helps to understand its culture, history and economic development.

## China by the numbers

:::definition
| Data | Figure |
|---|---|
| Area | 9.6 million km² (3rd or 4th largest depending on source) |
| Population | ~1.4 billion inhabitants (2025) |
| Capital | 北京 Běijīng (Beijing) |
| Largest city (population) | 上海 Shànghǎi (Shanghai) |
| Neighbours | 14 bordering countries (world record) |
| Official time zones | 1 only (UTC+8) although the territory spans 5 natural time zones |
| Coastline | ~18,000 km of coastline |
| Highest peak | 珠穆朗玛峰 Zhūmùlǎngmǎ Fēng (Everest) — 8,848 m — on the China-Nepal border |

China shares borders with: **Russia, Mongolia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Afghanistan, Pakistan, India, Nepal, Bhutan, Myanmar, Laos, Vietnam, North Korea**.
:::

## Major landforms and rivers

:::propriete
**Major landforms:**

| Landform | Character | Pinyin | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tibetan Plateau | 青藏高原 | Qīngzàng Gāoyuán | "Roof of the World" — average altitude 4,500 m |
| Gobi Desert | 戈壁沙漠 | Gēbì Shāmò | Large cold desert — Inner Mongolia / Mongolia |
| Taklamakan Desert | 塔克拉玛干沙漠 | Tǎkèlāmǎgān | Largest sand desert in Asia — Xinjiang |
| Northeast Plain | 东北平原 | Dōngběi Píngyuán | Large agricultural plain (corn, soya) |
| North China Plain | 华北平原 | Huáběi Píngyuán | Cradle of Chinese civilisation |

**The two great rivers:**

| River | Character | Pinyin | Length | Importance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Yangtze | 长江 | Cháng Jiāng | 6,300 km (3rd world) | Three Gorges Dam, southern economic axis |
| Yellow River | 黄河 | Huáng Hé | 5,464 km | "Mother of rivers", cradle of civilisation |

**The Yellow River** is called 母亲河 Mǔqīn hé (mother river) because the first Chinese dynasties developed on its banks.

## Beijing and Shanghai — the two capitals

:::methode
**北京 Běijīng (Beijing) — official capital:**
- Founded as a capital over 700 years ago (under the Yuan and Ming dynasties).
- Landmarks: 故宫 Gùgōng (Forbidden City), 长城 Chángchéng (Great Wall), 天安门 Tiān'ānmén.
- Language: 普通话 Pǔtōnghuà (Standard Mandarin) — Beijing is the birthplace of Mandarin.
- Character: political, academic, cultural. Seat of the central government.

**上海 Shànghǎi (Shanghai) — economic capital:**
- The world's busiest port (by container volume).
- Pudong special economic zone: iconic skyscrapers (Oriental Pearl Tower 东方明珠).
- Character: cosmopolitan, business, finance, fashion. Strong East-West blend.
- Shanghai is to China what Lagos is to Africa: the country's economic engine.

**Cultural difference**: Beijingers see themselves as guardians of culture and history; Shanghainese see themselves as pioneers of modernity.
:::

## The Africa-China connection: the modern Silk Road

:::attention
The **Silk Road** (丝绸之路 Sīchóu zhī lù), created in the 2nd century BCE, connected China to Europe via Central Asia. Today, the **"Belt and Road" initiative** (一带一路 Yīdài Yīlù, launched in 2013) is its modern version, including Africa.

In Cameroon, the modern Silk Road is concretely reflected in:
- The **Memve'ele dam** (Yaoundé–Ebolowa) — financed and built by Chinese companies.
- The **Kribi deep-water port** — co-financed by China.
- Roads and infrastructure in several regions.

Understanding China's geography helps explain **why** China is so interested in Africa: access to natural resources, commercial outlets, and geopolitical expansion.
:::
