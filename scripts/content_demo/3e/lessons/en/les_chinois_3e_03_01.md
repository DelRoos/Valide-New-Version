# Chinese traditional festivals

China has a rich heritage of traditional festivals, all linked to the **lunar calendar** (农历 nónglì). These festivals have punctuated the Chinese year for millennia and are occasions for family reunions, rituals and collective celebrations.

## The lunar calendar and the Chinese zodiac

:::definition
The **Chinese lunar calendar** (农历 nónglì, 'agricultural calendar') is based on moon cycles. It coexists with the solar Gregorian calendar (阳历 yánglì) used officially.

The **Chinese zodiac** (十二生肖 shí'èr shēngxiào) associates each year with one of **12 animals**: 鼠 rat, 牛 ox, 虎 tiger, 兔 rabbit, 龙 dragon, 蛇 snake, 马 horse, 羊 goat, 猴 monkey, 鸡 rooster, 狗 dog, 猪 pig. The full cycle lasts 12 years.

2025 = Year of the Snake (蛇 shé); 2026 = Year of the Horse (马 mǎ)
:::

## The four major festivals

:::propriete
| Festival | Character | Pinyin | Lunar date | Main traditions |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Spring Festival | 春节 | Chūnjié | 1st day of the 1st month | Family dinner, fireworks, red envelopes, dragon/lion dances |
| Lantern Festival | 元宵节 | Yuánxiāojié | 15th day of the 1st month | Coloured lanterns, riddles, tangyuan 汤圆 |
| Dragon Boat Festival | 端午节 | Duānwǔjié | 5th day of the 5th month | Dragon boat races, zongzi 粽子 |
| Mid-Autumn Festival | 中秋节 | Zhōngqiūjié | 15th day of the 8th month | Mooncakes, moon gazing |
:::

## The Spring Festival (春节) — the most important

:::methode
The Spring Festival is the Chinese equivalent of New Year, but celebrated according to the lunar calendar (between late January and mid-February).

**Traditions:**

1. **House cleaning**: chasing away the bad luck of the past year.
2. **Decorations**: red lanterns, spring couplets (春联 chūnlián) pasted on either side of the door.
3. **New Year's Eve dinner** (年夜饭 niányèfàn): the most important family meal of the year.
4. **Red envelopes** (红包 hóngbāo): giving money in red envelopes, a symbol of luck and prosperity.
5. **Fireworks** (烟火 yānhuǒ): to ward off evil spirits.
6. **Lion and dragon dances** (舞狮/舞龙 wǔ shī/wǔ lóng): in streets and markets.
7. **New Year's greetings** (拜年 bàinián): visits and greeting messages.
:::

**New Year greeting phrases:**

| Greeting | Pinyin | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 新年快乐！| Xīnnián kuàilè! | Happy New Year! |
| 恭喜发财！| Gōngxǐ fācái! | Wishing you prosperity! |

:::

## The Mid-Autumn Festival (中秋节)

:::definition
The **Mid-Autumn Festival** (中秋节 Zhōngqiūjié) falls on the 15th day of the 8th lunar month, when the moon is full and at its brightest. It is a festival of family reunion and moon gazing.

Symbols:
- 月饼 yuèbǐng (mooncakes): round pastries filled with lotus paste, red bean paste or salted egg yolk.
- 灯笼 dēnglong (lanterns): children parade with coloured lanterns.
- The legend of **嫦娥 Cháng'é**: a goddess who lives on the moon with her jade rabbit (玉兔 yùtù).
:::

## The Dragon Boat Festival (端午节)

:::propriete
The **Dragon Boat Festival** honours the poet-minister **屈原 Qū Yuán** (340–278 BCE), who drowned in the Miluo River to protest against corruption. Local people threw 粽子 zòngzi (glutinous rice wrapped in bamboo leaves) into the river to feed his spirit and prevent fish from eating his body.

Traditions:
- 龙舟比赛 lóngzhōu bǐsài = dragon boat races (drums, paddles)
- 粽子 zòngzi = stuffed glutinous rice cooked in bamboo leaves
:::

:::retenir
- Lunar calendar (农历 nónglì): the basis of all traditional festivals.
- Zodiac: 12 animals, 12-year cycle. 2026 = Year of the Horse (马 mǎ).
- **春节** Chūnjié: most important festival — family, 红包 red envelopes, fireworks, greetings.
- **中秋节** Zhōngqiūjié: full moon, 月饼 mooncakes, family reunion.
- **端午节** Duānwǔjié: dragon boats (龙舟), 粽子 glutinous rice.
- Key greetings: 新年快乐！(Happy New Year!) / 恭喜发财！(Prosperity!).
:::
