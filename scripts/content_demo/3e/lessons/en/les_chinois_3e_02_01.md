# The family (家庭 jiātíng)

Family holds a central place in Chinese culture. Respect for elders (长辈 zhǎngbèi) and family cohesion are fundamental values passed down since Confucius. Chinese makes very precise distinctions between family members — there is no single word for 'brother' or 'sister': you always specify whether the person is older or younger.

| Maternal cousin | 表兄弟/姐妹 | biǎo... | different family name |

Chinese distinguishes **paternal** (堂 táng) and **maternal** (表 biǎo) lines for cousins, and older/younger aunts and uncles from the father's or mother's side. This precision reflects the importance of family hierarchy.
:::

## Possession with 的 (de)

:::definition
In Mandarin, **possession** is expressed with the particle **的 de** placed between the possessor and the thing possessed.

Structure: **possessor + 的 + object**

Examples:
- 我的 wǒ de = my
- 你的 nǐ de = your
- 他的 tā de = his
- 她的 tā de = her
- 我们的 wǒmen de = our

Between close people (family, friends), 的 is often omitted:
- 我妈妈 wǒ māma = my mum (without 的 — intimate relationship)
- 我的老师 wǒ de lǎoshī = my teacher (with 的 — formal relationship)
:::

## Talking about your family

:::methode
**Useful phrases for introducing your family:**

1. **Number of people**: 我家有...口人 wǒ jiā yǒu... kǒu rén
   - 我家有五口人。Wǒ jiā yǒu wǔ kǒu rén. = My family has 5 people.
   - Note: 口 kǒu = specific classifier for family members

2. **Introducing someone**: 这是我的... zhè shì wǒ de...
   - 这是我的爸爸。Zhè shì wǒ de bàba. = This is my father.

3. **Asking questions**: 你有没有兄弟姐妹？Nǐ yǒu méiyǒu xiōngdì jiěmèi?
   - = Do you have brothers or sisters? (兄弟姐妹 = siblings collectively)
   - 我有一个哥哥和一个妹妹。= I have one older brother and one younger sister.
:::

:::exemple
**Amara introduces her family:**

我家有六口人。有爸爸、妈妈、爷爷、奶奶、一个哥哥和我。
Wǒ jiā yǒu liù kǒu rén. Yǒu bàba, māma, yéye, nǎinai, yī gè gēge hé wǒ.
My family has six people. There is my father, mother, paternal grandfather, paternal grandmother, one older brother and me.

我爸爸是老师。我妈妈是医生。我哥哥叫 Kevin，他二十岁。
Wǒ bàba shì lǎoshī. Wǒ māma shì yīshēng. Wǒ gēge jiào Kevin, tā èrshí suì.
My father is a teacher. My mother is a doctor. My older brother is called Kevin, he is twenty years old.
:::

:::attention
Confucius (孔子 Kǒngzǐ, 551–479 BCE) defined 5 fundamental relationships: ruler-subject, father-son, husband-wife, older brother-younger brother, friend-friend. These hierarchical relationships still influence how Chinese people address one another — you never call an elder directly by their first name without a title.
:::

:::retenir
- Chinese distinguishes older/younger for brother (哥哥/弟弟) and sister (姐姐/妹妹).
- Paternal grandparents: 爷爷/奶奶; maternal grandparents: 外公/外婆.
- Possessive: **possessor + 的 + object** (sometimes omitted between close people).
- Counting family members: **我家有...口人** (口 kǒu = classifier for family).
- Introducing: **这是我的...** (this is my...).
:::
